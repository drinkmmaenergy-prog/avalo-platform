# AVALO AML/KYC POLICY

**Version:** 1.0  
**Compliance:** EU AML Directives 2015/849, 2018/843  
**Last Updated:** November 7, 2025

## 1. PURPOSE

Prevent money laundering, terrorist financing, and fraud on Avalo platform.

## 2. KYC REQUIREMENTS

### Basic Users
- Email verification
- Phone (SMS) verification
- Age verification (18+)

### Earning Creators
- Identity verification (government ID)
- Live selfie check
- Address confirmation
- Enhanced due diligence for high earners

### High-Value Transactions
- Withdrawals >500 EUR equivalent require additional verification
- Source of funds documentation for large deposits

## 3. MONITORING

### Transaction Monitoring
- Automated screening for suspicious patterns
- Threshold alerts:
  - Single transaction >1,000 EUR
  - Daily total >2,000 EUR
  - Rapid deposit-withdrawal cycles

### Red Flags
- Structuring (breaking up transactions)
- Unusual transaction patterns
- High-risk jurisdictions
- Inconsistent user behavior
- Rapid account turnover

## 4. REPORTING

### Suspicious Activity Reports (SAR)
Filed with General Inspector of Financial Information (GIIF) Poland when:
- Suspected money laundering
- Terrorist financing indicators
- Fraud patterns

### Record Keeping
- Transaction records: 7 years
- KYC documents: 7 years after relationship ends
- SARs: Permanent

## 5. ENHANCED DUE DILIGENCE

Required for:
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
- High-risk countries (FATF list)
- Monthly earnings >10,000 EUR equivalent
- Suspicious activity flags

## 6. SANCTIONS SCREENING

Check against:
- EU sanctions lists
- UN Security Council lists
- OFAC (US Treasury)
- National lists

Matches result in immediate account freeze and report to authorities.

## 7. USER OBLIGATIONS

Users must:
- Provide accurate information
- Update info when changes occur
- Respond to verification requests within 14 days
- Report suspicious activity

Failure results in account suspension or termination.
