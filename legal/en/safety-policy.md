## Enforcement and Moderation Process

### 1. Automated Detection

#### AI Scanning Systems
Our AI systems continuously monitor:
- All public content (profiles, posts, photos, videos)
- Chat messages (only for pattern analysis, not content reading)
- Transaction patterns and financial anomalies
- User behavior and interaction patterns
- Known prohibited content signatures (PhotoDNA, hash matching)

#### Real-Time Alerts
High-risk content triggers immediate actions:
- Critical violations (CSAM, violence): Instant account suspension
- Suspicious patterns: Flagged for moderator review
- Cumulative violations: Progressive restrictions
- Geographic or network-based risks: Enhanced scrutiny

### 2. User Reporting

#### How to Report
Users can report violations through:
- In-app "Report" button on any profile, message, or content
- Email to abuse@avalo.app for serious concerns
- Crisis hotline at crisis@avalo.app for immediate threats
- DMCA takedown requests to dmca@avalo.app

#### What to Include
Effective reports should contain:
- Specific description of the violation
- Date and time of occurrence
- Screenshots or evidence (if safe to collect)
- Context or relevant background information
- Your desired outcome or concern

#### Reporter Protection
We protect reporters by:
- Keeping reports confidential (not shared with reported user)
- Not revealing reporter identity without consent
- Prohibiting retaliation against good-faith reporters
- Providing updates on report outcomes where appropriate

### 3. Human Review

#### Moderation Team
Our professional moderators:
- Review flagged content within 15 minutes to 24 hours
- Apply consistent standards based on policies
- Consider context and cultural nuances
- Escalate complex cases to senior reviewers
- Document decisions for transparency and appeals

#### Decision Factors
Moderators consider:
- Severity and impact of violation
- User's history and pattern of behavior
- Intent and context of the action
- Harm caused to other users or platform
- Applicable laws and regulations
- Platform safety and integrity concerns

### 4. Enforcement Actions

#### Progressive Discipline
For most violations, we apply graduated responses:

**First Offense:**
- Content removal + educational warning
- Explanation of policy violation
- Guidance on proper conduct

**Second Offense:**
- 7-day temporary ban
- Required review of community guidelines
- Warning about escalation

**Third Offense:**
- 30-day temporary ban
- Final warning before permanent action
- Account under enhanced monitoring

**Fourth Offense:**
- Permanent ban
- No further appeal available

#### Immediate Permanent Bans
No warnings for:
- CSAM or child exploitation
- Sex trafficking or solicitation of prostitution
- Violent threats or incitement
- Mass harassment campaigns
- Financial fraud at scale
- Repeated ban evasion

#### Account Restrictions
Partial limitations may include:
- Messaging restrictions (receive only, no sending)
- Content posting suspension
- Feature access revocation (live streaming, drops, etc.)
- Reduced discoverability or visibility
- Mandatory identity re-verification
- Transaction limits or holds

### 5. Communication

#### Notification of Actions
Users receive clear notifications about:
- Specific policy violated
- Evidence or example of violation
- Action taken (warning, ban duration, restrictions)
- Appeal process and deadlines
- Steps for reinstatement (if applicable)

#### Transparency
We provide:
- Explanation of moderation decisions
- Policy citations and relevant guidelines
- Aggregated enforcement statistics in transparency reports
- Regular updates on safety initiatives

## Appeals and Rights

### Your Right to Appeal

#### Eligible Appeals
You may appeal:
- Content removal decisions
- Account warnings or strikes
- Temporary or permanent bans
- Identity verification rejections
- Age verification disputes

#### Non-Appealable Decisions
No appeals for:
- CSAM-related bans
- Confirmed prostitution solicitation
- Violent threat enforcement
- Authenticated fraud cases
- Repeated ban evasion

### Appeal Process

#### Step 1: Submit Appeal
- Email appeals@avalo.app within 14 days of decision
- Include your username and decision reference number
- Explain why you believe the decision was incorrect
- Provide any additional context or evidence
- Request specific outcome

#### Step 2: Initial Review
Within 3 business days:
- Acknowledgment of appeal receipt
- Review of original moderation decision
- Verification of policy application
- Check for new evidence or mitigating factors

#### Step 3: Decision
Within 7 business days:
- Thorough review by senior moderation team
- Consideration of appeal arguments and evidence
- Final decision: uphold, modify, or overturn
- Detailed explanation provided

#### Step 4: Final Outcome
Possible results:
- **Appeal granted:** Ban lifted, account restored, apology issued
- **Appeal partially granted:** Ban reduced or restrictions modified
- **Appeal denied:** Original decision stands, no further appeals
- **Additional evidence requested:** Process extended for investigation

### Appeal Success Rates
Historically, approximately 12% of appeals result in changed decisions, demonstrating our commitment to fairness while maintaining strong enforcement.

## Data and Privacy Considerations

### What We Collect for Safety

#### Automated Collection
To maintain safety, we collect:
- Device information (type, OS, IP address)
- Location data (approximate, for fraud prevention)
- Usage patterns (login times, feature usage)
- Content metadata (upload times, edit history)
- Transaction records (for fraud detection)
- Behavior signals (flagged by AI systems)

#### User-Provided Data
We process:
- Profile information and photos
- Messages and chat logs (stored encrypted, analyzed for violations only)
- Reports and support communications
- Identity verification documents (securely encrypted)
- Payment information (processed by third parties)

### How We Use Safety Data

#### Purposes
Safety data is used exclusively for:
- Detecting and preventing violations
- Responding to reports and emergencies
- Improving moderation systems
- Complying with legal obligations
- Protecting users from harm
- Investigating fraud and abuse

#### Not Used For
We never use safety data for:
- Marketing or advertising
- Selling to third parties
- Unrelated commercial purposes
- Discriminatory profiling
- Political purposes

### Data Retention

#### Active Investigations
During active cases:
- Evidence preserved indefinitely
- Logs maintained for law enforcement cooperation
- Reports and communications archived
- Appeal records stored

#### Closed Cases
After resolution:
- Moderation records: 7 years
- Ban records: Permanent
- Warning records: 3 years
- Resolved reports: 1 year
- General safety logs: 90 days

### Data Sharing

#### Law Enforcement
We share data with authorities when:
- Legally required by valid subpoena or court order
- Reporting mandatory crimes (CSAM, trafficking)
- Preventing imminent harm or danger
- Investigating serious crimes
- Complying with national security requirements

#### Safety Partners
We share limited data with:
- NCMEC (National Center for Missing & Exploited Children) for CSAM reports
- PhotoDNA service providers for content matching
- Payment processors for fraud prevention
- Identity verification services
- Cybersecurity vendors for threat analysis

#### Never Shared
We never share:
- User identities with other users (reports are anonymous)
- Content of private encrypted messages
- Data beyond what's necessary for stated purposes
- Information for commercial exploitation

## Crisis Response

### Immediate Danger Situations

#### If You Are in Danger
üö® **Take these steps:**
1. Call emergency services (911 in US, 112 in EU)
2. Remove yourself from danger if safe to do so
3. Contact crisis@avalo.app immediately
4. Provide location and urgency details
5. Follow law enforcement instructions

#### Platform Response
When alerted to immediate danger:
- Escalate to senior safety team instantly
- Contact law enforcement with user location data
- Preserve all evidence and communications
- Provide user with emergency resources
- Follow up to ensure safety

### Suicide or Self-Harm

#### Warning Signs
If you observe:
- Direct threats or plans of self-harm
- Saying goodbye or giving away possessions
- Expressing hopelessness or worthlessness
- Sudden mood changes or calmness after depression
- Seeking means of self-harm

#### How to Help
üÜò **Immediate actions:**
1. Report to crisis@avalo.app immediately
2. Encourage them to contact crisis hotlines:
   - US: 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline
   - International: iasp.info for local resources
3. Do not leave them alone if possible
4. Remove access to means of harm if safe
5. Get emergency help (911/112) if imminent risk

#### Platform Response
We respond by:
- Immediate outreach to user with resources
- Connecting with mental health crisis services
- Notifying emergency contacts if available
- Preserving account to maintain support access
- Following up on user safety

### Domestic Violence or Assault

#### Support Resources
If you or someone you know is experiencing violence:
- National Domestic Violence Hotline: 1-800-799-7233 (US)
- National Sexual Assault Hotline: 1-800-656-4673 (US)
- Local law enforcement: 911 (US), 112 (EU)

#### Platform Actions
We support survivors by:
- Immediately banning abusive users
- Preserving evidence for legal proceedings
- Connecting survivors with victim advocacy
- Providing safe communication channels
- Respecting survivor autonomy and choices

### Human Trafficking

#### Identifying Trafficking
üö© **Warning signs:**
- User appears controlled by another person
- Inconsistencies in stories or coercion
- Signs of physical abuse or restriction

- Lack of control over finances or documents

#### Reporting
Report suspected trafficking to:
- National Human Trafficking Hotline: 1-888-373-7888 (US)

We take trafficking extremely seriously:
- Immediate reporting to NCMEC and law enforcement
- Cooperation with trafficking task forces
- Comprehensive evidence preservation
- Support for identified victims
- Training for moderators on identification
